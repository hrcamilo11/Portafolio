# Welcome to my digital space

Hello everyone! I am **Camilo Hernández**, a student of Systems Engineering and Informatics. Throughout my academic and professional career, I have been deeply immersed in a variety of fascinating disciplines, from backend development to data science and, most especially, cybersecurity.

I created this blog as a personal and professional corner to document my ongoing learning, share interesting software architectures, and break down the technical challenges I face every day.

![My ideal workspace](/blog/primera-entrada-header.png)

## A Multidisciplinary Approach

Today, building software isn't just about writing code; it's about **building secure, scalable, and intelligent systems**. In this blog, I will focus on the convergence of these three areas:

1. **Software Engineering**: We will discuss solid architectures, relational database optimization (like PostgreSQL and SQL Server), and building robust APIs using technologies such as Node.js, Spring Boot, and TypeScript.
2. **Data Science**: I will share my experiences extracting value from data, building visualizations, and applying models to solve real-world problems.
3. **Offensive Cybersecurity**: My area of greatest passion. We will explore _pentesting_ techniques, vulnerability analysis, and web application security.

![Abstract representation of a CTF](/blog/ctf-abstract.png)

## CTF (Capture The Flag) Competitions

If you spend time researching cybersecurity, you've surely heard of **CTFs**. As an active member of the hacking team **Ch0wn3rs**, I invest a significant amount of my time solving cryptography challenges, reverse engineering, basic exploding, and web security challenges.

In future posts, I will publish detailed **Write-ups**. My goal is not just to show "how" to capture the flag, but to explain the underlying vulnerability and how to mitigate it at the source code level. I firmly believe that the best way to learn how to defend a system is to understand exactly how it can be compromised.

## What can you expect in the coming months?

I've put together a content _roadmap_ that includes:
- **AWS Deployments**: How to take your local applications to a scalable Cloud environment.
- **Microservices vs Monoliths**: Lessons learned in real projects.
- **Social Engineering and Ethical Hacking**: Fundamental concepts to protect enterprise infrastructure.

I'm incredibly excited to begin this journey and I hope the content I share here will be highly useful to you, whether you're just starting in programming or looking to dive deeper into information security.
